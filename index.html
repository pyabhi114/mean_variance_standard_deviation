Calculate Mean, Variance, and Standard Deviation

A concise, step-by-step walkthrough with formulas and a working example.

By Abhishek Gogna - Professional Algo-Based Quantitative Research Analyst

Dataset

We will use the following data set of 5 numbers:

X = [4, 8, 6, 5, 3]

Step-by-Step Guide

Calculate the Mean

The mean (average) is the sum of all the numbers divided by the count of numbers.

Mean $= \\dfrac{\\sum X}{N}$

Mean $= \\dfrac{4+8+6+5+3}{5} = \\dfrac{26}{5} = 5.2$

Calculate the Variance

Variance measures the average of the squared differences from the mean.

Variance $= \\dfrac{\\sum (X_i - \\mu)^2}{N}$

  1. Subtract the mean from each value and square the result:
  • $(4 - 5.2)^2 = 1.44$
  • $(8 - 5.2)^2 = 7.84$
  • $(6 - 5.2)^2 = 0.64$
  • $(5 - 5.2)^2 = 0.04$
  • $(3 - 5.2)^2 = 4.84$
  1. Sum the squared differences and divide by $N=5$:

$1.44 + 7.84 + 0.64 + 0.04 + 4.84 = 14.8$

Variance $= \\dfrac{14.8}{5} = 2.96$

Calculate the Standard Deviation

The standard deviation is the square root of the variance and has the same units as the data.

$\\text{Standard Deviation} = \\sqrt{\\text{Variance}} = \\sqrt{2.96} \\approx 1.72$

Note: For a sample (not the full population), use $N-1$ in the denominator (this is the unbiased sample variance, often denoted $s^2$).

Summary

  • Mean: 5.2
  • Variance: 2.96
  • Standard Deviation: 1.72
